Our Story

We met while working as research assistants at CU Boulder in 2017. Just as things seemed poised to take off, Parke graduated and moved to Arizona. We knew our connection had too much potential to leave unexplored, so we took on the distance! By dint of selfie diaries, video calls, and airline miles, it worked, and we cultivated a rich and meaningful relationship. When COVID threatened to cut off the travel that sustained us, Murti flew to Arizona and we busted out of town to live off-grid in a 12' camper named the Rambling Gypsy Rose. Life in the Gypsy Rose cemented our love, and a year later we were engaged.

COVID-19 Precautions

Our Kentucky event will be part outdoors and part in a well-ventilated (read, old and gappy) barn, weather permitting, with about 60 people. Our Utah event will be entirely outdoors with about 30.

As of May 17, COVID is decidedly rising and hospitalizations are above the 15,000 threshold we set for masking. That means masks are part of the dress code -- at least when we pack in for the ceremony and dancing! Optional otherwise. Dining will be spaced. We will provide masks for everyone.

The current COVID hospitalization rate is viewable at Our World in Data.
