Our Story

We met while working as research assistants at CU Boulder in 2017. Just as things seemed poised to take off, Parke graduated and moved to Arizona. We knew our connection had too much potential to leave unexplored, so we took on the distance! By dint of selfie diaries, video calls, and airline miles, it worked, and we cultivated a rich and meaningful relationship. When COVID threatened to cut the travel that sustained us, Murti flew to Arizona and we busted out of town to live off-grid in a 12' camper named the Rambling Gypsy Rose. Life in the Gypsy Rose cemented our love, and a year later we were engaged.

COVID-19 Precautions

Our Kentucky event will be part outdoors and part in a well-ventilated (read, old and gappy) barn, weather permitting. Our Utah event will be entirely outdoors. If everyone RSVPs yes, we will have 120 guests at the Kentucky event and 70 at the Utah event, inclusive of partners and children and using our best guess for who will choose which location.

COVID is still very much with us, and we urge our guests to take appropriate precautions, both for our event and during their travel to and from.

If COVID spikes near either event, we will adapt or cancel. If nationwide hospitalizations exceed 15,000 (10% of peak) on the day prior to the event, we will stagger dining and masks (KN-95 or better) will become part of the dress code. If hospitalizations exceed 75,000 (50% of peak) one week ahead of the event or are projected to exceed this level by the time of the event, we will cancel. The current hospitalization rate is viewable at Our World in Data.
