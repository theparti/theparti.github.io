Our Story

We met while working as research assistants at CU Boulder in 2017. Just as things seemed poised to take off, Parke graduated and moved to Arizona. We knew our connection had too much potential to leave unexplored, so we took on the distance! By dint of selfie diaries, video calls, and airline miles, it worked, and we initiated and nurtured rich and meaningful relationship. When COVID threatened to cut the travel that sustained us, Murti flew to Arizona and we busted out of town to live off grid in a 12' camper named the Rambling Gypsy Rose. Life in the Gypsy Rose cemented our love, and a year later we were engaged.

COVID-19 Precautions

Our Kentucky event will be part outdoors and part in a well-ventilated (read, old and gappy) barn, weather permitting. Our Utah event will be entirely outdoors. We have invited (inclusive of partners and children)

That said, COVID is still very much with us, and we urge our guests to take appropriate precautions, both for our event and in their travel to and from.

If COVID spikes near our event, we might modify or cancel our event. If hospitalizations exceed 15,000 (10% of peak) on the day prior to the event, we will require that all guests wear KN-95 or better masks (we will do our best to provide them) and will stagger dining. If hospitalizations exceed 75,000 (50% of peak) one week ahead of the event or are projected to exceed this level by the time of the event, we will cancel the event. The current hospitalization rate is viewable at Our World in Data.

Gifts

Your presence during our celebration is a monumental gift in of itself! We kindly request that you do not gift us any items, as we're planning to continue our nomadic journey around the US and will not have the space. If you cannot make the wedding, there is one excellent gift you can give: a video! We would cherish a video with your love, memories, and blessings. If you insist on a material gift, you can donate to A Safe Place , a domestic violence center for women in Oakland, CA.
