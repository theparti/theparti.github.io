An Uncertain Start

They made a point to "see each other around."

They hiked Boulder's many trails, wrenched on Parke's aging Land Cruiser, and, with a pilot-friend, flew a small plane to Bryce Canyon to go backpacking. Through these experiences they came to recognize their mutual love of exploration, curiosity, and the wilderness. Despite this connection, Murti was in a relationship and Parke preparing to move to Arizona, so they carefully stayed well within the boundaries of friendship. When Parke was just a few weeks from his departure, Murti joined the goodbye party he threw at a cabin in the mountains. With friends, they spent a raucous weekend splitting firewood, tossing cabers, throwing hatchets, building fires, eating chili, and drinking from the "camel-keg." After everyone else left the next day, Parke and Murti stayed behind. For hours they lingered and talked in the quiet cabin, savoring what they expected to be the last of their time together.

But everything suddenly changed

one magical Tuesday night, when Parke decided to draw out the goodbye just a little more. When they met at a Boulder arcade, Murti revealed that she was newly single. With only a few days until his departure, Parke knew it was now or never, and they finally kissed. Was this just a rebound? Only time could tell.

They both felt the other was someone worth investing in, and they agreed to make a long-distance effort. Perhaps it would lead to a lifelong friend. Perhaps something different. As Parke journeyed across the rockies to a new life in Arizona, they kept up a dialogue, and began teaching themselves the art of the selfie.
